Embedding Models

Jul 2026 · 4 min read · AI | Speech

Most people talk about LLMs, diffusion models, and retrieval systems, but ignore speaker embedding models. That's a mistake.

Speaker embeddings compress vocal identity into a dense vector space where similarity is geometry. Think FaceNet, but for voice.

Modern systems like speaker verification pipelines use these embeddings for:

  • Voice authentication
  • Speaker diarization
  • Voice cloning conditioning

The interesting part is that the representation is not about speech content—it is about identity. You can say different sentences and still land in the same region of latent space.

This separation of "what is said" vs "who is saying it" is what makes them so powerful in real-world audio systems.
